Introduction


In this section, I want to explore female and male's workers percent to their total employment in different areas: agriculture, industry, service and self-employed. I chose the range of year from 2000 to 2019 to see the change of percent in different work status for different sex. In this way, users can get a direct impression and draw conclusion easily be themselves.

Agriculture

The below bar chart shows the average of female and male's agriculture workers' percent to their total employment of the whole 180 countries. Agriculture is the base of people's livelihood. No matter in developed or developing countries, agriculture workers must be enough to sustain a country's livelihood. So I averaged the 180 countries's data and show the change over years.


Findings

From the chart, we can see that male's agriculture workers percent of total employment is little higher than female's though the difference is very little. Over last 2 decades, the percent of agriculture workers is decreased constantly for both female and male. This means the live conditions for the whole world turns better and people have ability to choose more kind of works but not for just living.


Industry

Similiar to agriculture, industry shows the development of a country and requires enormouse workers to build. For example, in 2020, due to the spread of COVID-19, each country needs more hospitals, breathing mask production, nucleic acid test, etc. All these productions reflect a country's growth and resistance to natural disasters.


Findings

From the chart, we can easily see the male workers' percent in industry is about twice than female workers. The interesting thing is that over last 2 decades, male workers in industry percent keeps increasing but female workers in industry keeps decreasing. The result is that the difference between male and female workers percent in industry becomes larger and larger.


Service

Service worker is different from agriculture and industry workers. Service workers include cashier, food server, family service worker, etc. In contrast, service worker is not necessary for a development of a country. For example, Iraq suffers war over years so it does not require much service workers but need more industry workers to build. Basically, more developed countries contain more service workers. For this reason, I did not average the whole countries' servcice worker percent to total employment but chose 10 developed countries which are more typical for anaylsis.


Findings

From the chart, we can see in this time, female service worker percent is much higher than male workers' percent. Like the average percent of female service worker is about 90% but male is about 65%. And for the 10 countries, the percent of service workers is keep increasing especially for China, Japan and India. This condition shows that these countries are keep developing over years as they have more workers in service.


Self Employment

A self-employed person is an individual who earns a living by working for themself, not as an employee of someone else and not as an owner (shareholder) of a corporation. Self employed workers can hold their livings by wrting books, being freelancer, being taken account of by partner, etc. I picked this status into consideration because a large percent of females are forced to lose or pause jobs due to reproduction. Even after giving births to babies, a lot of women choose to give up working to take care of the children and become housewifes.


Findings

From the chart, we can find out that the percent of self-employed workers of female and male does not change too much. India has most self-employed females and males, the later one is China. The interesting thing is that in developed countries like Italy, Australia, self employed male workers are even more than self employed females. This is a sign of the rise of female right.
